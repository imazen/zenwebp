# Tuned-default adoption round 3: acting on the Pareto review (2026-07-16)

Follow-up to `rd_pareto_review_2026-07-16.md` — the review's levers, tried
on the tuned default (`CostModel::ZenwebpDefault`; parity untouched and
byte-verified per change). Harness: `dev/tuned_ab_sweep.rs` (now with
`--parity` and `--encoder libwebp` arms); corpus: the 15-image A/B set;
metric: matched-zsim size ratio (log-interp), `< 1` = candidate wins.
Raw data: `rd_round3_chroma_2026-07-16.tsv`.

## 4. Mid-row `level_costs` refresh at m3-m4 → **ADOPTED** — and it was the scans pocket

The per-MB decision probe (`dev/scan_stats_probe.rs`) showed the scans
gap was NOT mode decisions (I4/I16/skip counts identical ±10 of ~2800
MBs, segment quants ±1) — pointing at coefficient-cost tables. The one
untested tuned-vs-parity delta there: libwebp refreshes the level-cost
tables alongside the probabilities every `max_count` MBs; the tuned
default skipped it (a pre-parity-audit measurement had it as a
regression). Re-measured post-audit:

| axis | matched-zsim ratio |
|---|---|
| scans m4 (vs libwebp) | 1.024 → **1.002** |
| scans m6 (vs libwebp) | 1.042 → 1.003 (measured; NOT adopted at m6) |
| corpus m3 | **0.9983** |
| corpus m4 | **0.9985** (wins on 10/15 images) |
| corpus m6 | 1.0044 (LOSES on 10/15 images) |

Document scans have stationary token statistics — exactly where
image-adapted cost tables pay; the historical "regression" verdict did
not survive the parity audit's fixes at m3/m4, but remains real at the
trellis tiers (the refresh also feeds the trellis DP). **Adopted at
m3-m4 only**; m5-m6 keep pass-start tables (their gate verified
bit-identical, 1.0000). This closes the review's scans pocket at m4 and
shrinks it at m6 only via the tier choice (m4 now beats m6 on scans).

With rounds 1-3 landed the anchor stands at: **m0 0.908 / m2 1.005 /
m4 0.992 / m6 0.984** vs libwebp at matched zensim (m4 verified against
the anchor rows; m3 wins by the same margin over its baseline).
